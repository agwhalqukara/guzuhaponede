Michael Lerner net worth: How much was he worth?

Michael C. Lerner was an American actor. who was born on June 22, 1941,in Brooklyn, New York, United States; of Romanian-Jewish descent, the son of Blanche and George Lerner. He was raised in Red Hook, Brooklyn and in Solon, Ohio.

Michael Lerner made his first television appearance at the age of 13, as a “quiz kid” on a television program hosted by a local sportscaster. He played the role of Willy Loman in a production of Death of a Salesman at Brooklyn College, where Joel Zwick was a classmate. He also appeared as Sir Toby Belch in a production of Twelfth Night directed by David Mamet in Greenwich Village; William H. Macy was also in that production.

After graduating from Brooklyn College, where he studied acting, he received a scholarship to the University of California, Berkeley, where he earned a master’s degree. In 1968, Lerner returned to the San Francisco Bay Area and joined the American Conservatory Theater (A.C.T.). At the age of 24 he appeared as “Hieronymous the Miser” in a KPFA radio production of Michel de Ghelderode’s Breugelesque play, Red Magic.

He was nominated for the Academy Award for Best Supporting Actor for his role as Jack Lipnick in Barton Fink. He has featured in a lot of movies since 1968 and 2016. He died on the 8th of April, 2023.
